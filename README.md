# RAKSHA.live — Kerala Flood & Landslide Reporting Platform
🌧️ About The Project

RAKSHA.live is a real-time crowd-sourced disaster reporting platform built for Kerala’s monsoon season. The platform enables citizens to instantly report floods, landslides, blocked roads, rising water levels, and emergency situations directly from the ground.

Kerala has repeatedly faced devastating monsoon disasters — from the 2018 floods to the 2024 Wayanad landslides — where delayed information flow cost lives and slowed rescue coordination. RAKSHA.live focuses on solving one core problem:

People need verified ground truth faster than traditional alert systems can provide it.

The platform is designed as a Malayalam-first, mobile-friendly emergency reporting system where communities themselves become the fastest source of disaster intelligence.

# 🚨 Problem Statement

## During disasters:

Government alerts often arrive late
Ground situations change minute-by-minute
Remote villages lose communication access
Rescue teams lack hyperlocal visibility
Social media becomes chaotic and unverified

## RAKSHA.live aims to bridge that gap using:

Real-time citizen reports
Location-based incident mapping
Community-driven verification
Fast emergency visibility

## 🧠 Vision

RAKSHA is not just another reporting website.

It is an attempt to build a community-powered disaster intelligence network for Kerala.

The long-term vision includes:

Offline-first emergency reporting
AI-assisted risk detection
Live rescue coordination layers
Real-time hazard heatmaps
Integration with local authorities & NGOs
Multi-language accessibility
Disaster preparedness ecosystem


## 🎯 Design Goals

The platform was built with these priorities:

Speed over complexity
Clarity during panic situations
Mobile usability in low-connectivity areas
Minimal learning curve
Fast reporting workflow
High accessibility

During disasters, users do not have time to “figure out” interfaces.
Every interaction must be obvious and immediate.

## 📖 Real-World Context

Kerala has faced repeated extreme weather disasters:

2018 Kerala Floods
2019 Monsoon Flooding
Multiple Western Ghats landslides
2024 Wayanad Landslide tragedy

These events caused massive displacement, infrastructure destruction, and loss of life across the state.

## RAKSHA.live was inspired by the need for:

faster citizen communication,
localized emergency visibility,
and decentralized disaster reporting.
